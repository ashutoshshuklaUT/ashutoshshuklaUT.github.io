<p><strong style="font-size: 1.5em;">Research Background</strong></p>

<p>I completed my PhD in Operations Research and Industrial Engineering at UT Austin, where I developed large-scale optimization frameworks for infrastructure planning under uncertainty. I designed stochastic and robust optimization models and implemented scalable solution methods, including parallelized decomposition algorithms, to solve high-dimensional network problems efficiently. My research has been published in peer-reviewed venues and received the Best Student Paper Award at the IISE Annual Conference. In addition, I also conducted research in reinforcement learning for sequential decision-making in networked systems, including inverse reinforcement learning for routing policy inference and multi-agent reinforcement learning for coordinated traffic optimization.</p>
